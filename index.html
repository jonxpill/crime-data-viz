MISDAADVELD Western Cape crime as a field of light

MISDAADVELD

Afrikaans: “crime field” · Western Cape, 2008/09 – 2025/26

Every glowing dot is one reported crime. Eighteen years of South African Police Service records for the Western Cape — robbery, residential burglary, murder, sexual offences, commercial crime and carjacking across 150 police precincts — drawn as a single living field of ~180,000 points. The province, its districts, the pies, the terrain and the monthly pulse are all the same dots rearranging; nothing is ever redrawn, only moved.

Glow is crime; grey is geography. Colour is local density — cool and sparse through molten and dense. Click a district to bloom into it. N breathes month by month, C shows rates per 100k people, T raises the land itself.

Honesty notes. Counts are real; positions are jittered within each precinct (volume is true, street addresses are not). Raw counts largely follow population — per-capita (C) is the fairer read (denominators: WorldPop 2020). 2025/26 is the sum of SAPS’s quarterly releases and is unaudited; the monthly series (Apr 2021 – Mar 2026) comes from the same quarterlies. Stations opened after 2023 (Samora Machel, Makhaza) are folded into the older precinct boundaries that contain them. Carjacking is also a subcategory of aggravated robbery, so it appears inside the robbery lens too. And throughout: these are reported crimes — what reaches a police station, not all that happens.

Crime: SAPS Annual Crime Records 2008–2023 via DataFirst (CC-BY) + SAPS annual & quarterly releases 2023–2026 (saps.gov.za). Boundaries: Western Cape Government GIS. Population: WorldPop 2020. Terrain: AWS Terrain Tiles. Built on three.js with a custom GPU point-field engine — one buffer of points, every view a pure layout function. By Jonx Pillemer.
